Understanding Sanatana Dharma

Three Aspects of Practice

We explore Sanatana Dharma through three fundamental dimensions that span the cosmic, divine, and spiritual — each offering a unique lens into ancient Indian knowledge systems.

01

Primary Focus

Adhi Bhauthika

A rare and thoroughly scientific practice relating not only to the world but also to cosmic sciences. This aspect makes our work unique and deeply rooted in scientific understanding — connecting earthly phenomena with universal principles.

02

The Divine Aspect

Adhi Daivika

The divine dimension of Sanatana Dharma practices, connecting spiritual wisdom with cosmic consciousness and universal energies. A bridge between human experience and the greater forces that govern existence.

03

The Spiritual Dimension

Adhyatmika

The innermost spiritual dimension of ancient practices, focusing on inner consciousness and the journey toward self-realization — the ultimate purpose of all Sanatana Dharma traditions and teachings.

Who We Are

Mission & Vision

To conserve, document, and celebrate India's traditional and cultural sciences through systematic research, heritage conservation, educational programs, and community partnerships.

We envision a world where India's traditional and cultural sciences are recognized, valued, and integrated into contemporary life — vibrant communities that honor their heritage while embracing innovation.



Our discourses are shaped through discussions with Historians, Spiritual Gurus, Resource Persons, Scientists, and Cultus — bringing together diverse perspectives for a comprehensive view of ancient heritage.

Our Work

Impact Areas

From sacred architecture to ancient manuscripts, our work spans the full breadth of India's living heritage.

Temple Architecture & Sacred Spaces

Restoration and documentation of ancient temples, sacred groves, and architectural marvels that embody India's spiritual and artistic heritage across centuries.

Traditional Arts & Crafts

Supporting artisans and craftspeople in preserving traditional techniques in textiles, metalwork, pottery, painting, and other heritage crafts at risk of disappearing.

Ancient Manuscripts & Texts

Digitization, translation, and preservation of ancient manuscripts containing knowledge on medicine, astronomy, philosophy, and cultural practices of India.

Ritual Sciences & Practices

Documentation and study of traditional rituals, ceremonies, and practices that form the living, breathing heritage of Indian communities across regions.
